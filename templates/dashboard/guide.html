  Usage Guide

Principals

In this app, the base for any school is the principal. Principals can signup using the signup button provided in the homepage.

After clicking on the signup button, you'll be redirected to a page. On this page, you'll be asked to fill in your details. Note that picture and email are optional fields. You can add emails and picture later in your profile page. When you fill in your sign up form and click on the signup button, you'll be redirected to the another page where you'll need to tell us the name of your school. And just then your sign up process will be over and you'll ve redirected to the homepage again, where you can enter the actual app by clicking on the dashboard button.

In the dashboard, you'll face a sidenav with several options provided for you. Each of them will lead you to a different part of the app. We'll go through all of those pages together here.

Dashboard

Starting with the dashboard itself. The dashboard is the first page, which gives you an overview of your school. Which consists of the name of your school, the number of classes you have, the number of teachers, and the number of students. A table consisting of 5 classes of your school is also provided. You can click on the class name to view details about the class, it's subjects, and students. Click on the three dots and you'll be redirected to the classes page. Also, we have provided another table for the teachers. You can click on the teacher name to view details about the teacher. This table is also provided with a three dots button to redirect to the teachers page.

Classes

When you come to this page, you'll see a table that lists your classes. By clicking on the class ID of each class, you'll be again redirected to another page, that shows the details of that class. As an example, consider the class "901" in the picture.

We click on the class ID and then we we're taken to the following page which shows us the subjects added to the class and the students. There are also two buttons at the top of the page by clicking on which you can edit or remove your class.

In order to create a new class, you need to click on the "New" button which is located at the top of the classes page. by clicking on this you'll be represented a form which you need to fill in in order to create your class.


This form includes two types of fields. The first one asks for the name of your class, or in other terms your school-unique class ID. The second one asks for the subjects that are supposed to be included in your class. It's named "Courses" because we encounter the subject and its teacher together and it feels more correct to call it just "Courses" instead of "Subjects". After filling out the form, click on the create button and your class will be created.

Teachers

The next section is the teachers section. Here teachers are shown in box-like elements that represent an overview of them.


By clicking on the "Add" bottom on the top of the page you'll be shown a form where you can add a new teacher. If you click on those box-like elements, you'll be taken to a page with the details about that teacher. This page gives you the teacher's name, username, email, university, and their degree. You can change the university and the degree but the email can only be changed by themselves. There's a table containing the classes. you can visit the detail the detail page of the class by clicking on the class id. There's also another table representing the courses that this teacher is the teacher of.

Courses

This page is the courses page. Here you can see an overview of all the existing courses.

By clicking on the "New" button at the top of the page you'll be shown a form where you can add a new course. You'll be asked to enter the name of the course, and select its teacher.

By clicking on the box-like elements you'll be redirected to the detail page of the course.

Students

This page is the students page. Here you can see an overview of all the existing students and register new students. The mechanism is like the other pages we went through. There is a box-like element for each student. By clicking on the box-like element you'll be redirected to the detail page. And there is an "Add" button at the top of the page. By clicking on this you'll be shown a form where you can add a new student.

Teachers

Teachers can only be signed up by the principal of their school. Ask your principal to sign you up, set you a username and a password. You can change your password afterwards in case you don't want others to have it.

The sidenav is a little different for teachers. Teachers have access to exams page, whereas principal don't. Classes page and students page are also briefer for teachers. They only have access to the details of the classes they teach and students in those classes.

Dashboard

The dashboard page is different for teachers. It first shows you two charts. The one on the left chart, gives you an overview of for each of your classes in a certain month. By clicking on the "Classes" button on the top of the chart, you'll be represented a dropdown where you can select the class you want to see the overview for. On the other hand, the chart on the right chart gives you an overview of all of the classes during a certain month.


Classes

The page is a laconic overview of all the classes you teach. Unlike other pages, you won't be taken to any page if you click on the class ID.


Students

You are shown box-like elements with brief details about your students. You can click on them to see the detail page of the student.


On the detail page, you'll see your student's email (if set), the class they are in, their username, and their average grade in all of their exams.

Exams

This page might need a little more explanation. Like other pages, your data is represented in a box-like elements. By clicking on the box-like element you'll be redirected to the detail page. In this page, there are two buttons on the top of the page. One is used to add a new exam. It represents you a form, where you need to specify the class you want to add the exam to, after you select the class, another field will show up that asks you to select the subject. The next field asks you to enter the full score, which is 20 by default, and has to be a number. The next field asks you to enter the date of the exam. Click on the field and a date picker will show up. Use the date picker to select the date of the exam. And finally, there's a toggle button that asks if the exam should be visible to students.


The other button is used to filter the exams. By clicking on it, you'll be presented a modal where you can select the class you want to see the exams for, the subject you want to see, and the date you want to see. The date is a date range actually, but you can use one without the other one.


Click on the elements and you'll be taken to the detail page, where you can set grades for the students in the exam by clicking on the add/edit button.

Students

This user type does not need any specific explanation. Students can only access the exams page, assignments page, the articles page, the profile page, the messenger, and the dashboard page. These are the pages that all users have access to.

All The Other Pages

Profile

This is your profile page. The same for all the user types. You can change your password, change your email, and change your bio. Your bio is used in the messenger.

Messenger

The internal messenger. The only user type that can create groups is the principal. The principal can create groups by clicking on the "New Group" button on the top of the page, where he'll be asked to enter the group name. They can upload profile pictures and set a group bio as well, but it's completely optional. After they created the group, they'll be redirected to the group page. For adding members, click on the group name, which is located in a frame on the top, scroll down to the "Members" section. Cilck on "Add Members" and you'll be presented a modal where you can select the members whether by searching their username or name or by finding the users manually. If you're using this app from Iran, you need to have your VPN on in order to add members since Algolia is not available in Iran. Other users can create conversations where they also need to have their VPN on to find the user they want to text.

Articles

All the teachers and principals can add articles. The articles are shown on the articles page. By clicking on the "New" button, you'll be redirected to a page where you can write your article. This form asks you for a title, categories that are comma separated (You can't have more than 5 categories and categories can't have more than 20 characters, e.g. "Math, Physics"), and the article itself. You can make use of tools provided in the editor to write your article. You can also use LaTeX. To do so, click on the Math button, which is located in the editor by a summation sigma symbol.

Assignments

Only teachers can add assignments. The assignments are shown on the assignments page. The form used for adding assignments is almost the same as the one used for adding articles. Just that you need to set the assignment class, subject, and the deadline. The editor for the body is the same as the one used for articles.
